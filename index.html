Weekly Weight Loss Plan – 1500 kcal Target

Structured meals, alternating lunches, and basement-friendly workouts.

Weekly Calendar Breakfasts Lunches Dinners Snacks Workout Plan

Overview

Target for Jill: ~1500 kcal/day with high protein and structured days. Two different lunch batches are made on Sunday and then alternated through the week.

Rough daily structure:

  • Breakfast: ~350 kcal
  • Lunch: ~400–430 kcal
  • Dinner: ~450–520 kcal
  • Snacks: ~200–250 kcal

Equipment used for workouts: treadmill, dumbbells, bench + bar, floor mat. Often: one person on the treadmill while the other does floor/core or dumbbell work, then swap.

Breakfasts

B1 – Banana Protein Shake

~350 kcal • ~25–30g protein

  • 1 medium banana (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 scoop whey or similar protein powder
  • 1 tbsp peanut butter or 2 tbsp PB powder
  • 1/2 cup oats
  • 1 cup unsweetened almond milk
  • Ice + water to texture
  1. Add all ingredients to blender.
  2. Blend until smooth and thick. Adjust with water/ice as needed.

B2 – Warm Protein Oats

~350–375 kcal • ~25–30g protein

  • 1/2 cup oats
  • 1 scoop vanilla or cinnamon protein powder
  • 1/2 banana (sliced) or 1/2 cup berries
  • 1 tsp brown sugar or sweetener (optional)
  • 1 tsp chia or ground flax (optional)
  • Water or almond milk to cook
  1. Cook oats with water/almond milk per package directions.
  2. Remove from heat, stir in protein powder thoroughly.
  3. Top with fruit and chia/flax; add sweetener if desired.

B3 – Greek Yogurt Power Bowl

~350–380 kcal • ~25–30g protein

  • 3/4–1 cup non-fat or low-fat Greek yogurt
  • 1/2 cup berries or sliced fruit
  • 2 tbsp granola or crushed cereal
  • 1 tbsp chopped nuts or seeds
  • Optional: drizzle of honey or sugar-free syrup
  1. Add yogurt to a bowl.
  2. Top with fruit, then granola and nuts/seeds.
  3. Drizzle a small amount of honey/syrup if desired.

Lunches (Both Batches Made Sunday)

L1 – Turkey Enchilada Skillet Bowls (5 servings)

~420 kcal per serving • ~45g protein

Ingredients

  • 2.5–3 lbs 93% lean ground turkey
  • 2 cups cooked rice (about 1 cup dry)
  • 2 cans black beans, rinsed
  • 1 can fire-roasted diced tomatoes
  • 1 can red enchilada sauce (~10 oz)
  • 2 cups frozen corn (or 1 can, drained)
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 2–3 tbsp taco seasoning (or chili powder, cumin, garlic, paprika, salt)
  • 1 cup light Mexican cheese
  • Optional: cilantro, green onion, jalapeño, lime

Instructions

  1. Cook rice and set aside.
  2. In a large skillet, sauté diced onion until soft.
  3. Add ground turkey, cook until browned; drain excess if needed.
  4. Stir in taco seasoning and cook 1–2 min.
  5. Add beans, tomatoes, corn, and enchilada sauce; simmer 5–10 min.
  6. Stir in cooked rice and adjust seasoning.
  7. Turn off heat, sprinkle cheese over top, cover for 3–5 min to melt.
  8. Divide evenly into 5 containers. Add cilantro/green onion if using.

Serving: Reheat and eat as-is or with shredded lettuce on the side.

L2 – Mediterranean Lentil Bowls (5 servings)

~400–430 kcal per serving • ~20–25g protein • high fiber

Ingredients

  • 2 cups dry lentils (green or brown), rinsed
  • 4 cups low-sodium broth + water as needed
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1–2 bell peppers, diced
  • 1 cucumber, diced (kept separate until serving)
  • 1–2 cups cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta (optional)
  • 2–3 tbsp olive oil total
  • Juice of 1–2 lemons
  • Seasoning: oregano, cumin, salt, pepper, paprika

Instructions

  1. In a pot, sauté onion and garlic in a bit of olive oil until softened.
  2. Add lentils and broth; bring to a boil, then simmer until tender (about 20–25 min). Add water if needed.
  3. Stir in diced peppers and seasonings; cook a few more minutes.
  4. Turn off heat, stir in lemon juice and 1–2 tbsp olive oil.
  5. Let cool slightly, then portion lentils into 5 containers.
  6. Top each with tomatoes, cucumber, and a bit of feta (or add fresh daily).

Serving: Eat warm or room temp. Add a squeeze of lemon at serving.

Dinners (Single-Meal, Easy to Scale)

Teriyaki Salmon + Veggie Stir Fry

~500 kcal per person

  • 5–6 oz salmon per person
  • Stir-fry veg (broccoli, carrots, snap peas, etc.)
  • 2–3 tbsp light teriyaki sauce total
  • Optional: small serving of rice (adjust calories as needed)
  1. Roast or pan-sear salmon (about 10–12 min depending on thickness).
  2. Stir-fry veggies in a pan with a little oil or spray; add teriyaki sauce last minute.
  3. Serve salmon over veggies; drizzle remaining sauce.

Turkey Meatballs + Marinara + Roasted Veg

~450–500 kcal per person

  • 5–6 turkey meatballs per person (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1–1.5 cups roasted veg (zucchini, mushrooms, peppers)
  • 1/2–3/4 cup marinara
  • Optional: sprinkle of Parmesan
  1. Bake or pan-cook meatballs per package/recipe.
  2. Roast vegetables on a sheet pan at 400°F until tender and browned.
  3. Warm marinara and pour over meatballs; serve with roasted veg.

Fast Taco Night (Lean Beef)

~450–480 kcal per person (depends on tortillas and toppings)

  • ~1/2 lb lean ground beef shared between two (or 4–5 oz each cooked)
  • Lettuce cups or small tortillas
  • Salsa, onion, cilantro, shredded lettuce
  • Optional: light cheese, Greek yogurt instead of sour cream
  1. Brown beef, season with taco seasoning.
  2. Serve in lettuce cups or tortillas with toppings.

Greek Turkey Bowls

~480 kcal per person

  • 5–6 oz cooked turkey (grilled or pan-cooked)
  • 1/2 cup cooked rice
  • Tomato, cucumber, red onion (diced)
  • 2 tbsp tzatziki per person
  • Optional: olives, a bit of feta
  1. Cook turkey with simple seasoning (salt, pepper, garlic, oregano).
  2. Layer bowl: rice, turkey, chopped veg, tzatziki, optional feta/olives.

Sheet Pan Shrimp Fajitas

~450 kcal per person (with 2 small tortillas)

  • Shrimp (~6–8 oz per person)
  • Sliced bell peppers and onions
  • Fajita seasoning, oil or spray
  • Small tortillas or lettuce wraps
  1. Toss shrimp and veg with oil and seasoning.
  2. Roast on sheet pan at 400°F for 8–12 min (watch shrimp closely).
  3. Serve in tortillas or lettuce with salsa/lime.

Mini Personal Flatbreads

~500 kcal per person (depends on base)

  • 1 small naan or lavash per person
  • 2–3 tbsp marinara
  • 1/4–1/3 cup light mozzarella
  • Turkey pepperoni or cooked turkey sausage
  • Optional: veggies (mushrooms, peppers, onions)
  1. Preheat oven to 400°F.
  2. Top naan/lavash with sauce, cheese, and toppings.
  3. Bake 8–10 min until crisp and melty.

Chili + Side Salad

~500 kcal per person

  • Lean ground turkey or beef
  • Canned tomatoes, beans, onions, chili seasoning
  • Side salad with light dressing
  1. Cook meat with onion; add tomatoes, beans, seasoning, and simmer.
  2. Serve a hearty bowl with a simple side salad.

Snack Options

Aim for 1–2 snacks per day in the 100–200 kcal range.

  • Protein bar (check label, many are 170–200 kcal)
  • Hummus + veggies (carrots, peppers, cucumber)
  • Greek yogurt (plain or light flavored)
  • Apple + 1 tbsp nut butter

Workout Plan (Basement Setup)

Goal: ~30 min mornings, with optional 10–15 min evenings (except busy nights Wed & Fri). You have a treadmill, dumbbells, bench + bar, and a floor mat. Often: Person A = treadmill while Person B = core/dumbbells, then swap.

Standard AM Structure (30 min)

  • 5 min: Warm-up walk on treadmill.
  • 15 min: Strength block (upper or lower focus).
  • 10 min: Cardio block on treadmill.

Example Strength Blocks

Lower Body (Mon/Thu)

  • Goblet squats (dumbbell)
  • Romanian deadlifts (dumbbells or bar)
  • Reverse lunges or split squats
  • Glute bridges or hip thrusts (on bench)

2–3 sets of 8–12 reps each. While one person lifts, the other walks at a brisk pace, then swap.

Upper Body (Tue/Fri)

  • Bench press or floor press
  • One-arm dumbbell rows
  • Dumbbell shoulder presses
  • Biceps curls + triceps extensions

Same structure: 2–3 sets of 8–12 reps per movement.

Full Body / Mixed (Wed/Sat)

  • Dumbbell squats
  • Dumbbell rows
  • Push-ups (bench or floor)
  • Hip hinge (RDL) or deadlift variation

Core Block (10–15 min)

Pick 4–5 moves and cycle through for 2–3 rounds:

  • Dead bugs
  • Planks (front/side)
  • Bird-dogs
  • Glute bridges
  • Leg lowers or marches

While one person is doing this circuit, the other can do a 10–15 min treadmill block, then swap roles.

Cardio Blocks

  • Easy/Moderate: 10 min at a pace where talking is possible but not effortless.
  • Intervals (Sat example): 1 min faster / 2 min easy, repeat 4–5 times.
